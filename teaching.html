How I Teach

I believe engineering education should connect theory to practice. In my courses, I emphasize hands-on problem solving and real-world applications, helping students see how the mathematics and algorithms they learn translate into systems that process images, secure communications, and analyze signals. I want every student to leave my classroom not just with technical knowledge, but with the confidence to apply it.

Courses

ECE 241: Signals and Systems

Fall Junior

Introduction to continuous and discrete time signal theory and analysis of linear time-invariant systems. Signal representations, systems and their properties, LTI systems, convolution, linear constant coefficient differential and difference equations, Fourier analysis, and discrete-time processing of continuous-time signals.

ECE 245/445: Wireless Communications

Spring Junior

This course teaches the underlying concepts behind traditional cellular radio and wireless data networks as well as design trade-offs among RF bandwidth, transmitter and receiver power, and system performance. Topics include channel modeling, digital modulation, channel coding, network architectures, medium access control, routing, and cellular networks. Students complete a semester-long research project to gain in-depth experience with wireless communications and networking.

ECE 287/487: Strategic Entrepreneurship & Innovation for Engineers

Fall Junior Advanced Elective

This course introduces engineering students to the fundamental principles of entrepreneurship, focusing on transforming technical ideas into real-world solutions that address people's needs. Students learn practical skills such as identifying potential users, understanding their challenges, and developing solutions that align with those needs. Through discussions, case studies, and projects, students develop a broader problem-solving perspective and understand what it takes to sustain and support their ideas over time.

ECE 348: Design Seminar

Fall Senior

Students explore design project ideas, form teams, and develop proposals for their senior capstone. Through presentations and documentation, they clarify project requirements and create a plan that establishes the foundation for their year-long design experience.

ECE 349: Design Capstone

Spring Senior

The capstone senior design course brings together everything students have learned across their ECE curriculum. Students scope, design, build, and present an original engineering project under faculty mentorship. Prior faculty approval or a design project proposal is required.
