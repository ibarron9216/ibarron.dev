Teaching Philosophy

How I Teach

I believe engineering education should connect theory to practice. In my courses, I emphasize hands-on problem solving and real-world applications, helping students see how the mathematics and algorithms they learn translate into systems that process images, secure communications, and analyze signals. I want every student to leave my classroom not just with technical knowledge, but with the confidence to apply it.

Courses I Teach

Courses

ECE 241: Fundamentals of Electrical Engineering

Fall 2024 Introductory

This introductory course covers the essential principles of electrical engineering, including circuit analysis, signal representation, and basic systems concepts. I guide students through foundational topics that prepare them for advanced study in signal processing, communications, and computer engineering.

ECE 245/445: Digital Signal Processing

Spring 2025 Upper-Level

In this course, I teach the principles of digital signal processing including discrete-time signals and systems, the Z-transform, discrete Fourier transform, and digital filter design. Students work with practical applications in audio, image processing, and communications through hands-on projects and MATLAB exercises.

ECE 487: Senior Design Project

Spring 2025 Senior

The capstone senior design course brings together everything students have learned across their ECE curriculum. I mentor teams as they scope, design, build, and present an original engineering project. I also integrate entrepreneurship and innovation concepts, helping students connect their technical work to real-world impact.
