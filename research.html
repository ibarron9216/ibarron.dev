What I Study

My research lies at the intersection of signal processing, security, and privacy. I develop techniques for digital image forensics — detecting manipulated or forged images — and explore how visual data can be protected without sacrificing usability. This work has applications in law enforcement, journalism verification, and secure communication systems.

I also investigate barcode technologies and privacy-preserving communication channels, designing systems that balance transparency with confidentiality. My goal is to create practical tools that make digital media more trustworthy and secure.

Grants

National Science Foundation · $50,000

An I-Corps Approach to Developing a Commercial QR Code Scanning Solution

with Prof. Gaurav Sharma

This NSF-funded project explores the commercialization potential of secure QR code scanning technology. Through the I-Corps program, I worked to validate market needs and develop a pathway from academic research to a practical product that helps users identify malicious QR codes before they cause harm.
