What I Study

My research focuses on securing visual communication and authentication through advanced barcode and QR code technologies. I develop dual-modulated QR codes that embed private data visible only at close distances, enabling secure communication in public settings without compromising standard QR code functionality. This work addresses critical security challenges in an increasingly connected world.

I also apply image processing and forensic techniques to detect QR code attacks and authenticate digital media using blockchain-based systems. My goal is to create practical security solutions that protect digital assets, from detecting malicious QR codes to authenticating fine art paintings, while maintaining usability and accessibility for legitimate users.

Grants

National Science Foundation · $50,000

An I-Corps Approach to Developing a Commercial QR Code Scanning Solution

with Prof. Gaurav Sharma

This NSF-funded project explores the commercialization potential of secure QR code scanning technology. Through the I-Corps program, I worked to validate market needs and develop a pathway from academic research to a practical product that helps users identify malicious QR codes before they cause harm.
