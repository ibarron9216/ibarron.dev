Overview

I work at the intersection of security, authentication, and emerging technologies. My research explores how clever encoding, blockchain, and signal processing can solve real-world trust and verification problems. Rather than building new systems from scratch, I leverage existing platforms—QR codes, barcodes, image forensics—and enhance them with security properties that matter in practice.

A key focus is proximity-based security: embedding private information in visual codes that decode only at close range, enabling secure public communication without extra infrastructure. I've also explored blockchain-based authentication for digital provenance, from verifying fine art paintings to detecting tampered QR codes. The common goal across this work is bridging the gap between theoretical security and practical deployment—creating solutions that are both secure and usable.

More recently, I'm exploring how artificial intelligence can enhance these security systems and accelerate the path from research to real-world impact. I'm interested in the entrepreneurial side of security research: how to take innovations in authentication and privacy and translate them into products and services that solve problems for real users.

An I-Corps Approach to Developing a Commercial QR Code Scanning Solution

with Prof. Gaurav Sharma

This NSF-funded project explores the commercialization potential of secure QR code scanning technology.
